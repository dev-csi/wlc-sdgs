About WLC SDGs

Theme: providing a voice and a platform to the grassroots women enabled by the 4th industrial revolution

Following the ratification of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals by United Nations Member States in the year 2015, there is a promise of prosperity for the younger generation and our women worldwide. However, it is the duty of Governments, Business Sector, Civil Society, Academia, Media and all citizens to ensure that the dream of a Sustainable Development is realized by 2030. Sustainable Development is a Right to Humanity in itself as seen in the Universal Declaration on Human Rights, the International Convention on Social and Political Rights and the International Convention on Economic and Cultural Rights.


There is no doubt that the world has the adverse effects of climate change, population growth and violations around human rights before coming up with a strategy to curb the same. It is further evident that women are the most affected by lack of development especially in the Least Developed and the Developing Nations, whereas Gender equality is one of the 17 Goals that the world hopes to achieve by the year 2030. It is for this reason that the Women Leadership Conference on Sustainable Development Goals will be convening women leaders across the world to promote partnerships and development for women in the areas of Sustainable Development Goals.


The approach to engage the rural women directly is a win-win, and needs to be deliberate and strategic. The agenda is to activate and create a platform for the lower level of the pyramid woman to engage and participant in the achievement of SDGs not only for herself, but for the entire good of the community and society. Even though it is assumed that the Women Leaders that are more often than note.

WLC-SDGs Rational and Context

Technology is no longer a tool for the elite, especially given that data (information) is king in the 4th Industrial Revolution context. If mobile penetration in all the communities is anything to go by, technology is no longer a preserve of the few and the obtaining advancement, if properly deployed, would be a transformative phenomenon. In an effort to empower women, we have often viewed that educated and corporate women as the epitome of success in women empowerment. This notion of top-layer success has always left the gender discourse to be dominated by the elite women. There is no doubt that SDGs target the lower level of the pyramid women major catalysts of development and of SDGs have to be achieved, the economic grassroot women must be not just be given a voice, but a steady platform that deviates from occasional opportunities as a worker and entertainer in the Global South.


In appreciating the Theory of Change, the grassroot women must be viewed as the leader and not made the leader. Her views must be the views that inform policy for development. The elite women must be viewed as facilitator and not the leaders. It is the role of the elite women to provide a genuine platform for the grassroots women to be organic participants of social and economic change. Regardless of which SDGs in question, the grassroots women must play the lead role, and elite women as facilitators and providers of relevant information (useful data). The provision of accurate data or information to grassroots women has been made easier with acceleration of the 4th Industrial Revolution phenomena. In the simplest of terms, 4th Industrial Revolution is data on faster digital platforms for smart and intelligent processes. This data reaches the grassroot women much faster than before. The women already ave smart devices such as basic smart phones through which they can access information and share information.


Technology will undoubtably reengineer socio-economic transformation in alignment to the theory of change which looks at the impact-based engagements driven by grassroots women enabled by new and emerging technologies. The realization of SDGs by 2030 is a function of Women, all women, realizing their full potential, not as a mere movement, but as productive individuals. 17 SGDs must be unpacked for the grassroots women in Africa.

WLC-SGDs Objectives

  1. Provide a platform for collaboration for women leaders towards SDGs realization in Africa
  2. Leverage technology to promote women participation towards achieving SDGs
  3. Generate strategies for streamlining women involvement in SDGs work in Africa
